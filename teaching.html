Teaching

Teaching has always been one of my favorite parts of academic life. I especially love engaging with others who question and challenge my views of the world. I've helped design and teach courses across a pretty wide range of topics, all connected by a common interest in how economics intersects with cities, policies, and people.

The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania

REAL/BEPP 208/708 Housing Markets
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2018, 2020, 2021  ·  Prof. Joe Gyourko

A U.S.-focused course on the economics and operations of housing markets, covering supply and demand fundamentals, affordability, zoning, homebuilders, rental landlords, and the single-family rental sector, housing cycles, and a comparative analysis of Chinese housing markets.

REAL/FNCE 730 / BEPP 773 Urban Fiscal Policy
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Fall 2019  ·  Prof. Fernando Ferreira

A course on how cities provide public services — covering why cities exist, when fiscal policy fails, and local government realities including inequality, crime, congestion, and unfunded pensions. Special attention to housing affordability policies like rent control and inclusionary zoning, enterprise zones, infrastructure investments, and public-private partnerships.

BEPP 265/765 Economics of Diversity and Discrimination
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2022  ·  Prof. Corinne Low

An evidence-based look at economic inequality across race and gender, guided by economic theory and empirical research. Topics include workplace discrimination, policies to promote gender equality, the historical evolution of economic gender roles, women's empowerment in developing countries, measurement of racial bias, and how understanding hidden barriers can improve firm equity and performance.

REAL 375/875 Real Estate Disruptions
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Fall 2019  ·  Prof. Gilles Duranton

An examination of how technology is reshaping the real estate industry — from financing and development to construction, management, and leasing — and how it will likely transform the way real estate is used and designed in the future.

REAL 205/705 Global Real Estate
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2019  ·  Prof. Maisy Wong

An introduction to global real estate markets with a focus on income-producing assets, covering valuation, risk management, currency and political risk, rule of law, property rights, and cultural factors across markets outside the United States.

WH 297 Wharton Public Policy Industry Exploration
Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2018  ·  Prof. David Zaring

A selective, application-based program exposing students to the public policy sector through research seminars in Washington, D.C. Host organizations included the U.S. Treasury, Congressional Budget Office, RAND Corporation, International Finance Corporation (World Bank Group), and Bloomberg Government.
