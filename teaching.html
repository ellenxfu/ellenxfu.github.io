Teaching

Teaching has always been one of my favorite parts of academic life. I especially love engaging with others who question and challenge my views of the world. I've helped design and teach courses across a pretty wide range of topics, all connected by a common interest in how economics intersects with cities, policies, and people.

The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania

REAL/BEPP 208/708 Housing Markets
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2018, 2020, 2021  ·  Prof. Joe Gyourko

A U.S.-focused course on the economics and operations of housing markets, covering affordability, zoning, homebuilders and rental landlords, housing cycles, and comparative analysis of international markets. The material connects directly to debates playing out in every American city.

REAL/FNCE 730 / BEPP 773 Urban Fiscal Policy
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Fall 2019  ·  Prof. Fernando Ferreira

How cities raise and spend money — covering why cities exist, when fiscal policy fails, infrastructure investments, and local government realities including inequality, crime, housing policy, and public-private partnerships. Closely related to my dissertation research.

BEPP 265/765 Economics of Diversity and Discrimination
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2022  ·  Prof. Corinne Low

An evidence-based look at economic inequality across race and gender, guided by economic theory and empirical research. Topics include workplace discrimination, the historical evolution of economic gender roles, measurement of racial bias, and how understanding hidden barriers can improve firm equity and performance.

REAL 375/875 Real Estate Disruptions
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Fall 2019  ·  Prof. Gilles Duranton

An examination of how technology is reshaping the real estate industry — from financing and development to construction, management, and leasing — and how it will likely transform the way real estate is used and designed in the future.

REAL 205/705 Global Real Estate
MBA & Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2019  ·  Prof. Maisy Wong

An introduction to global real estate markets with a focus on income-producing assets, covering valuation, risk management, currency and political risk, rule of law, and cultural factors across markets outside the United States.

WH 297 Wharton Public Policy Industry Exploration
Undergraduate  ·  Spring 2018  ·  Prof. David Zaring

A field-based course exposing students to the public policy sector through research seminars at agencies including the U.S. Treasury, Congressional Budget Office, RAND Corporation, and the World Bank.
