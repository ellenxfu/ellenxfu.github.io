Teaching

Teaching has always been one of the parts of academic life I loved most — not lecturing, exactly, but the back-and-forth of helping someone work through a hard idea until it clicks. I've taught and designed courses across a pretty wide range of topics, all connected by a common interest in how economics intersects with cities, policy, and people.

The Wharton School, University of Pennsylvania

Housing Markets MBA & Undergraduate · Spring 2018, 2020, 2021

The economics of housing supply and demand, with attention to affordability, zoning, neighborhood change, and the policy levers available to local governments. One of my favorite courses to teach — the material connects directly to debates playing out in every American city.

Urban Fiscal Policy MBA & Undergraduate · Fall 2019

How cities raise and spend money, with a focus on property taxes, public services, and the political economy of local government finance. Closely related to my dissertation research.

Economics of Diversity and Discrimination MBA & Undergraduate · Spring 2022

An evidence-based look at economic inequality across race, gender, and other dimensions — how discrimination operates in labor markets, housing, and credit, and what the data tells us about causes and remedies.
