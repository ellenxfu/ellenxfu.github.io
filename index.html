Ellen Fu, PhD

Economist. Strategist. Believer that markets should work better for everyone.

Hi 👋 I'm Ellen. I'm an applied economist with over a decade of experience using data, causal inference, and economic reasoning to understand how markets, institutions, and policies shape cities and people.

My research interests sit at the intersection of urban economics, housing, and public policy. My dissertation focused on studying how property taxes burden homeowners and how racial gaps in commuting have persisted across decades. That work wasn't just academic for me. It reflected a genuine belief that careful, rigorous analysis is a precondition for meaningful change.

After finishing my PhD at Wharton, I joined Cornerstone Research, where I lead teams on complex antitrust, competition, and merger matters for Fortune 500 clients. The work has sharpened my ability to structure hard problems quickly, communicate across technical and non-technical audiences, and manage high-stakes projects under pressure.

Alongside my consulting work, I've been increasingly engaged with the economics of AI — how it's reshaping labor markets, housing, consumer finance, and the regulatory landscape. These are the same questions I've always cared about, playing out now at a new scale and speed.

I've lived in Florida, Philadelphia, New York, and am now based in San Francisco. Outside of work, I enjoy staying active, hanging out with cool people, reading, and making trip itineraries.
